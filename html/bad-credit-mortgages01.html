Does an overdraft impact credit score?

An overdraft is a form of borrowing that could be authorised or unauthorised. It needs to be considered as an emergency fund, not as an expansion of your bank accounts which you access frequently.

Used responsibly, an authorised overdraft should not affect your credit score. Should you maintain it pretty up to the limitation for an elongated time period, but then it might have an effect on a creditors decision.

Employing an unauthorised overdraft to pay payments when you do not have the money will undoubtedly have a negative influence on your credit score.

In case you've got a massive overdraft facility set up, even if you do not utilise it much at all, it still might have an effect on how much you can borrow for other purposes.
